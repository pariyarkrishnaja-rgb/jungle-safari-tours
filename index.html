Born of the Sal Forest

"I grew up when Chitwan was still a silent forest. There were no concrete hotels—just our traditional Tharu mud-and-thatch houses standing under the stars."

Krishna Pariyar (born 1981) represents the heart of Chitwan’s pioneer guiding lineage. His father was one of the very first guides in the region, introducing travelers to the wild long before roads crossed the plains. Krishna learned the language of the forest as a boy, tracking rhino prints in the dust and listening to the alarm calls of deer.

Since officially guiding in 2007, Krishna has committed his life to both hosting travelers and actively preserving Chitwan's wildlife. He is frequently recruited for the national tiger census counting operations, bird surveys, and river ecosystem monitoring projects.

A Win-Win Model: Jungle Safari Tours has no corporate middleman. It is run directly by local guides who live in Chitwan. 10% of all profits go directly into Tharu community skill enhancement, funding training for the next generation of guides and conservationists.

Our Commitment

A Promise of Zero Animal Exploitation

True luxury lies in witnessing animals in their wild, undisturbed habitats. Chitwan has suffered from commercial tourism that exploits elephants and other beasts. We run a different, deeper kind of journey.

Strict Ethical Rules We Stand By

No Elephant Riding

We refuse to offer elephant back safaris. Riding elephants causes physical harm and requires cruel training methods. We walk on our own feet, keeping a respectful distance.

No Animal-Drawn Carts

We do not use horse carriages or ox carts. We believe no animal should work for tourist transportation. We walk or use modern vehicles for transfer.

100% Wildlife Dignity

Our guides are trained to read distress signals. We never corner, trace, or make noise to provoke animals for photo-ops. The jungle belongs to them.
